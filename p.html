<p>During mid-August 2013 parts of eastern <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> and northeastern <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> were stricken by heavy flooding. At least 85 people died from the floods and more than 105 others were left missing as of August 19.<sup id="cite_ref-Xinhua85_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Xinhua85-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> More than 60,000 homes were destroyed and 840,000 people evacuated from <a href="/wiki/Heilongjiang" title="Heilongjiang">Heilongjiang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jilin" title="Jilin">Jilin</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Liaoning" title="Liaoning">Liaoning</a> provinces due to flooding which happened at the same time as flooding in China's southern <a href="/wiki/Guangdong" title="Guangdong">Guangdong</a> province.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a 2x" data-file-width="4608" data-file-height="3456" /></a><figcaption>Khabarovsk-Komsomolsk Highway, September 1</figcaption></figure> <p>From the end of July to mid-August 2013, unusually heavy rainfall occurred near the <a href="/wiki/Amur_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Amur River">Amur River</a>, which marks the dividing line between China and Russia. Starting on August 10, 2013, areas of northeastern China began to experience flooding.<sup id="cite_ref-DW_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DW-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> From August 15 to 17, heavy rainfall worsened the problem, causing the worst flooding in the region in more than a decade.<sup id="cite_ref-Xinhua85_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Xinhua85-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Times_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Times-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Nankouqian Township, one of the hardest-hit areas, saw 44.9 centimetres (17.7&#160;in) of rain, half the average annual total, on August 16 alone.<sup id="cite_ref-havoc_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-havoc-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> By August 18, water levels at 61 reservoirs surpassed the "danger" level. <a href="/wiki/Fushun" title="Fushun">Fushun</a> city in the <a href="/wiki/Liaoning_province" class="mw-redirect" title="Liaoning province">Liaoning province</a> was especially hard hit as rainstorms caused several rivers in the city to overflow.<sup id="cite_ref-Times_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Times-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Across the border in eastern Russia, heavy flooding was also reported, with <a href="/wiki/Amur_Oblast" title="Amur Oblast">Amur Oblast</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jewish_Autonomous_Oblast" title="Jewish Autonomous Oblast">Jewish Autonomous Oblast</a> and <a href="/wiki/Khabarovsk_Krai" title="Khabarovsk Krai">Khabarovsk Krai</a> the hardest hit.<sup id="cite_ref-DW_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DW-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> More than 140 towns were affected by what Russia authorities described as the worst flooding in 120 years.<sup id="cite_ref-ABC_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ABC-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The Amur River reached a record 100.56 metres (329.9&#160;ft), surpassing the previous record set in 1984, and was still rising as of August 19, threatening to flood the major city of <a href="/wiki/Komsomolsk-on-Amur" title="Komsomolsk-on-Amur">Komsomolsk-on-Amur</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-havoc_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-havoc-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ABC_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ABC-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> </p><p>In China, more than 60,000 homes were destroyed and numerous roads were blocked or damaged. More than 787,000 <a href="/wiki/Hectare" title="Hectare">hectares</a> of farm land were ruined in the region which depends heavily on farming.<sup id="cite_ref-Xinhua85_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Xinhua85-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Power and communications lines were downed in several townships.<sup id="cite_ref-Times_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Times-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Total damaged was estimated at 16.14 billion yuan (approx. US$2.6 billion/€1.94 billion).<sup id="cite_ref-Xinhua85_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Xinhua85-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In Russia, 3.2 billion rubles (approx. US$97 million/€73 million) was allocated for relief efforts.<sup id="cite_ref-DW_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DW-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> </p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Casualties">Casualties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_China%E2%80%93Russia_floods&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Casualties"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>China's <a href="/wiki/Liaoning_province" class="mw-redirect" title="Liaoning province">Liaoning province</a> was the hardest hit with 54 reported deaths and 97 people missing as of August 19, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-Xinhua105_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Xinhua105-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/Jilin_province" class="mw-redirect" title="Jilin province">Jilin province</a>, 16 deaths were reported. <a href="/wiki/Heilongjiang_province" class="mw-redirect" title="Heilongjiang province">Heilongjiang province</a> experienced 11 deaths.<sup id="cite_ref-havoc_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-havoc-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Across the region 360,000 people were displaced and 3.74 million affected in some way.<sup id="cite_ref-Xinhua85_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Xinhua85-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> No casualties were reported in Russia, but 20,000 people were evacuated. Two captive brown bears were rescued via helicopter.<sup id="cite_ref-ABC_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ABC-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> </p><p>Unrelated flooding resulting from <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Utor_(2013)" class="mw-redirect" title="Typhoon Utor (2013)">Typhoon Utor</a> in south China occurred simultaneously, causing the death tolls from the two floods to be combined in official reports. Typhoon Utor floods killed at least 33 people.<sup id="cite_ref-Xinhua105_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Xinhua105-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> </p> <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Relief_efforts_and_aftermath">Relief efforts and aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_China%E2%80%93Russia_floods&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Relief efforts and aftermath"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> <p>In Russia, more than 30,000 volunteers helped distribute 53 tons of food and supplies to flood victims. Officials are considering delaying the mayoral elections in Amur which are scheduled for September 8. A decision on the elections will be made August 27.<sup id="cite_ref-ABC_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ABC-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> </p><p><a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="Communist Party of China">Chinese Communist Party</a> <a href="/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Communist_Party_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="General Secretary of the Communist Party of China">general secretary</a> <a href="/wiki/Xi_Jinping" title="Xi Jinping">Xi Jinping</a> called for "all-out efforts" as relief work got underway.<sup id="cite_ref-Xinhua85_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Xinhua85-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> More than 120,000 people, including 10,000 soldiers, helped with relief and rescue efforts.<sup id="cite_ref-havoc_9-3"
